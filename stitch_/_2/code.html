Detailed Explanation

The principle of legality, encapsulated in the maxim nullum crimen, nulla poena sine praevia lege poenali, fundamentally dictates that individuals cannot be penalized for conduct that was not criminalized at the exact time it was committed.

Why Option B is correct: It accurately describes the core tenet of non-retroactivity, which protects citizens from arbitrary state power by ensuring laws are prospective. This prevents the state from retroactively altering the legal consequences of past actions to the detriment of the accused.

Why your answer (Option A) is incorrect: The prohibition is primarily concerned with substantive changes—specifically the definition of crimes and the severity of punishments. Procedural changes often can be applied retroactively, making A factually backward.
